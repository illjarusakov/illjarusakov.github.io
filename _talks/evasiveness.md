---

Consider the following problem: given a graph property P on graphs with n vertices, how much information about the presence of the n-choose-2-many edges do we need, to determine whether the graph lies in P or not? To formulate this problem rigorously, we will introduce the notion of query complexity and treat some graph properties. The above question will be discussed for some examples, motivating the Evasiveness Conjecture. Finally, we will examine the conjecture for small n.
