 In the 1960s, Graham Higman discovered a celebrated result now known as
 the Higman embedding theorem, which establishes a stunning relationship
 between an algorithmic property of groups and a natural algebraic class of
 groups. Our goal is to define this algorithmic property of being recursively
 presentable and then to outline a proof of the theorem. In the
 end we will look at some applications, conjectures and active research on
 this matter.
